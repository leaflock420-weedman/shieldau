Know Your Rights
General information only — not legal advice
Right to Silence

You generally have the right to remain silent and not answer questions that may incriminate you. You should still provide your name and address when lawfully required under the Police Powers and Responsibilities Act 2000 (Qld).

Drug & Alcohol Testing

You must comply with lawful directions to provide a breath, blood, or saliva sample. Queensland roadside drug testing detects THC, methamphetamine, and MDMA. Refusing a lawful test is a separate offence with serious penalties.

QLD has zero-tolerance THC in saliva — prescription is not a defence to drug driving.
Search & Seizure

Police may search you, your vehicle, or property only with lawful authority — e.g. a warrant, your consent, or specific powers under PPRA.

Complaints & Footage

Complaints: QPS Ethical Standards Command or Crime and Corruption Commission (CCC). You may apply for body-worn camera footage through QPS.

Right to Silence

Under the Law Enforcement (Powers and Responsibilities) Act 2002 (NSW), you must provide your name and address in certain situations. You generally do not have to answer other questions or participate in an interview beyond identification requirements.

Drug & Alcohol Testing

You must comply with lawful breath, blood, oral fluid, or urine tests. Refusing is a serious offence. NSW has introduced a medicinal cannabis defence for oral fluid THC in limited circumstances — subject to strict conditions including not being impaired (law reformed 2023–2024; check current thresholds).

Always comply with testing. Do not rely on this summary — laws are complex and change.
Search Powers

Police searches in NSW require lawful authority under LEPRA — e.g. warrant, arrest, reasonable suspicion (personal search), or vehicle search powers. You can ask: "Am I under arrest?" and "What is the reason for the search?"

Custody & Legal Help

If detained, you have the right to contact a lawyer. NSW Police must provide reasonable facilities. Legal Aid NSW provides advice and duty lawyers at court. Custody managers oversee your welfare in custody.

Complaints

NSW Police Force complaints: Law Enforcement Conduct Commission (LECC) for serious misconduct; NSW Police Professional Standards Command for other complaints.

Right to Silence

Under the Crimes Act 1958 (Vic) and Summary Offences Act 1966 (Vic), you must provide name and address when lawfully required. You generally may refuse to answer further questions or participate in a recorded interview without legal advice.

Drug & Alcohol Testing

You must comply with preliminary and evidential breath tests and oral fluid tests. Victoria Police have published discretion guidelines for medicinal cannabis patients in some circumstances — but compliance with testing is still mandatory and charges remain possible.

Never refuse a lawful test. Seek specific legal advice if you hold a medicinal cannabis prescription.
Search & Arrest

Searches require powers under Victorian law — warrant, arrest, or specific statutory powers. If arrested, you have the right to communicate with a friend, relative, or lawyer (subject to police procedures).

Legal Assistance

Victoria Legal Aid provides advice and duty lawyers. After-hours bail service available in some circumstances. For police interviews, request a lawyer before answering questions.

Complaints

Complaints about Victoria Police: IBAC (Independent Broad-based Anti-corruption Commission) for serious matters; Victoria Police Professional Standards Command for other conduct issues.

Reviewed by lawyers (demo label). Laws change — speak to a qualified lawyer for advice specific to your situation.

Medicinal Cannabis (QLD)
General information only — laws change frequently
Driving in Queensland

Queensland has a zero-tolerance approach to THC in saliva for licence holders. A valid prescription does not provide a defence to drug driving offences in QLD. You can be charged even if you used cannabis days earlier and are not impaired.

Police Interactions

If stopped, you must comply with lawful directions including saliva testing. You may need to show your prescription or pharmacy label if cannabis is found — but this does not prevent a drug driving charge.

Workplace

Employers may have drug policies that apply regardless of prescription. General information only — workplace rights depend on your contract, industry, and specific circumstances.

Other states (brief)

NSW has introduced a THC threshold for medicinal cannabis patients (subject to conditions). VIC has discretion guidelines. WA and QLD remain stricter. Always check current law in your state.
